Overactive Bladder: ‘Something to Live With’?

Making Clinically Significant Change for Your Older Patients with OAB

A free accredited CME program by mdBriefCase

Program Information

  • Overactive bladder (OAB) is a bothersome condition with a significant impact on quality of life. As the prevalence of OAB increases in association with age, it is important to manage symptoms effectively to maintain patient function and quality of life.


  • Management in older patients presents special challenges. Recent reviews and guidance recommendations for this patient population will help guide the safe management of OAB in elderly, including frail elderly individuals.


  • This program will explore clinical practice considerations in two case-scenarios of older patients who are ‘living with’ their OAB symptoms.

Learning Objectives for this Course

Upon completion of this program, participants will be able to:

Engage patients in discussion about bladder symptoms, to identify opportunities and agree on a goal of therapy for

effectively managing OAB.
Tailor modifiable variables and strategies to achieve and sustain beneficial treatment effects in managing OAB.
Intervene optimally to treat OAB in older patients, with individualized approaches that maximize safety and efficacy.
