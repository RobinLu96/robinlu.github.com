Insulin Devices

– Their Increasing Role in Diabetes Management

A free accredited CME program by mdBriefCase

Program Information

  • The Canadian Diabetes Association estimates that by 2020, one in three Canadians will be living with diabetes, undiagnosed diabetes or prediabetes.1 In the past, insulin therapy was normally only considered for patients with type 1 diabetes. With the progressive nature of type 2 diabetes, insulin therapy is being utilized as an option for an increasing percentage of this patient population.

  • Several decades ago, clinicians only had the choice of pork or beef insulin and the use of a traditional syringe to deliver insulin therapy. Not only has insulin therapy been transformed through the use of human insulin and the development of newer insulin analogues, the delivery of insulin has gone through a major change with the introduction of improved, insulin devices that make it easier for patients to deliver accurate doses of insulin as well as other injectable medications used in diabetes.

  • When clinicians consider insulin therapy for a patient with diabetes, it is not only important to consider the insulin type, but also the optimal insulin delivery device that best addresses the needs of the individual patient.

Learning Objectives for this Course

Upon completion of this program, participants will be able to:

Discuss the pros and cons of the insulin delivery devices most commonly used in Canada.
Review the importance of engaging the patient in the selection of the insulin delivery devices.
Explore the role for insulin pens and pumps in the management of patients with diabetes.
Examine how to customize the insulin delivery device for patients with diabetes.
